Scholarships

Details about available scholarships and how to apply.

  • Types of scholarships
  • Guidelines

    • Types of scholarships

    Students at the Lodz University of Technology can apply for financial assistance in the form of:

  • social scholarship
  • scholarship for disabled people
  • financial aid
  • rector's scholarship

    • Scholarship guidelines

    1. What scholarships are available for students?


    Available scholarships include social, academic, rector’s, sports, and scholarships for students with disabilities. Detailed information is available on the university website or at the dean's office.


    2. What documents are required to apply for a social scholarship?


    Required documents include, among others, a certificate of family income, a certificate of family size, and other documents confirming the financial situation (e.g., administrative decisions, tax returns).


    3. What are the criteria for awarding an academic scholarship?


    An academic scholarship is awarded based on academic results, typically based on the average grade from the previous semester and academic achievements. The required average grade may vary depending on the faculty.


    4. When and where are scholarship funds paid?


    Scholarships are paid to students' bank accounts, usually at the beginning of each month. Details about payments can be found in announcements on the university website.


    5. Are scholarships available for part-time students?


    Yes, scholarships are available for part-time students as well, although some types of scholarships may have additional criteria related to the study format.


    6. How can I check the status of my scholarship application?


    Information about the status of your application is sent directly to the student’s email address.


    7. Can I appeal the decision regarding my scholarship application?


    Yes, you can appeal the decision regarding the granting or denial of a scholarship within the time specified in the scholarship regulations. Appeals are submitted to the scholarship committee.
